Course Reflection

Reflective Insights on Problem Solving

  • How do you determine the most efficient approach when solving a complex problem? The approach depends on analyzing time complexity, space complexity, and constraints of the problem. I compare various algorithms and pick the most optimized one based on the given requirements.
  • Reflect on a situation where you need to balance multiple conflicting constraints in a design. When designing a system with limited resources, I prioritized critical features first while ensuring non-essential components remained functional, adopting a trade-off strategy to maximize the system’s efficiency.
  • What criteria do you use to evaluate the effectiveness of a solution? I evaluate a solution by considering correctness, efficiency, maintainability, and simplicity. For instance, the correctness of sorting algorithms can be validated by their ability to handle edge cases and large datasets.
  • How can you adapt an existing solution to address a new or unforeseen challenge? Solutions can be adapted by identifying bottlenecks or areas where a new constraint requires optimization, such as adapting a batch-processing algorithm to real-time streaming data.
  • What strategies do you use to identify patterns or structures in complex datasets or problems? I use clustering algorithms like K-means to identify patterns, especially in large datasets, which can help uncover hidden relationships and structures.
  • How do you decide when to prioritize simplicity over optimization in a solution? Simplicity is prioritized when the problem is small-scale, or when optimizing complexity adds little value compared to the overhead of more complicated solutions.
  • Reflect on how breaking down a problem into smaller components can help you approach it more effectively. Breaking problems into smaller sub-problems allows for better manageability, easier debugging, and systematic solution design.
  • Reflect on the trade-offs while choosing between different approaches to solve a problem. A balance needs to be struck between the efficiency of the algorithm and its complexity. For example, dynamic programming is more accurate but may have higher space complexity.
  • How do you identify and address potential limitations or weaknesses in a proposed solution? Testing edge cases and performance bottlenecks helps identify limitations, allowing me to refine the solution further.
  • Reflect on how applying knowledge from one context can help you solve a problem in a different context. Knowledge from graph algorithms can help solve network routing problems, as both deal with optimal pathfinding and connectivity issues.
  • How do you decide when to innovate versus relying on tried-and-tested solutions? Innovation is necessary when existing solutions fail to meet new requirements or constraints, but I rely on established solutions when they are efficient and scalable enough.
